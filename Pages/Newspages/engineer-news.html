    Engineer's day: Celebrating Revolution and Ingenuity

    The birth anniversary of one of India's greatest engineers, M Visvesvaraya is celebrated as Engineer's Day. Know about the engineer, scholar, and statesman. Mokshagundam Visvesvaraya, popularly called 'Sir MV' was one of India's finest engineers. The birth anniversary of M Visvesvaraya, on September 15, is celebrated as Engineer's Day in India every year.

    He was born in 1861 to a Telugu family at Chikkaballapur, a little over 55 km from Bengaluru. Both his parents were well-known Sanskrit scholars. He was an expert in irrigation techniques and flood disaster management. As a fine engineer and statesman, he was made part of a committee constituted in 1917 to advise on the future of Indian States. He was the architect of the Krishnaraja Sagar Dam, one of the biggest dams in India. In 1955, M Visvesvaraya was awarded India's highest civilian honour, the Bharat Ratna for his immense contribution to the development of the country. Various engineering institutes and organizations celebrate this day by organizing completions and felicitating students for their remarkable achievements in the field of Engineering..
